1. WHAT ALIGN WORKSPACES IS
================================================================================

Align Workspaces is a marketplace platform based in Miami, FL that connects
two types of users:

HOSTS: People who own or lease professional workspaces (therapy offices,
salon stations, coaching rooms, wellness studios, creative spaces) and want
to rent them out by the hour during idle time.

GUESTS: Independent professionals (therapists, barbers, coaches, real estate
agents, wellness practitioners, tattoo artists, photographers, etc.) who need
flexible, profession-appropriate workspace to see clients without signing a
traditional lease.

Align is a marketplace intermediary. We do not own, operate, or manage any
workspace. When a host and guest enter into a booking, they enter into a
direct contractual relationship with each other. Align facilitates the
connection, processes payment, and provides trust and safety tools.

The arrangement between host and guest creates a revocable license to
occupy, not a lease or any interest in real property.

The platform also operates a portrait photography service managed by the
founding team, with its own booking, gallery, and payment flows.

2. HOW THE PLATFORM WORKS (END TO END)
================================================================================

LISTING FLOW:

- Host creates an account (magic link email authentication, passwordless)
- Host uploads proof of insurance ($1M+ general liability required)
- Carrier name, policy number, coverage type, coverage amount, expiration date, declarations page (PDF or image)
- This is a hard gate: no listing can be published without it
- Hosts without insurance are directed to Thimble (partner) where they can purchase general liability starting at $17/month
- Host creates a listing with space details, pricing, photos, amenities, availability
- Listing is reviewed by Align team before going live
- Host can optionally self-certify their space under one of five Professional Use Certification tiers (see Section 5)
- Host can create an Arrival Guide with step-by-step photos, WiFi credentials, and door codes

BOOKING FLOW:

- Guest browses available spaces, filters by type, neighborhood, price, certification
- Guest selects date, time, and duration
- System checks availability (prevents double-booking via advisory locks and external calendar sync)
- Booking fee breakdown is displayed:
- Base price (host's hourly rate x hours)
- Guest service fee: 7% (5% for repeat guests)
- Sales tax: 7% (Miami-Dade County)
- Total charged to guest
- Booking Agreement is presented (see Section 4)
- Guest confirms and proceeds to Stripe checkout
- Payment is collected via Stripe
- Booking is confirmed, both parties notified via email and push notification
- Host receives payout automatically via Stripe Connect minus applicable fees

FEE STRUCTURE:

- Host fee: 12.5% standard, 10.5% for host-referred bookings, 0% for Founding Hosts (first 20 spaces, promotional)
- Guest fee: 7% standard, 5% for repeat guests
- Sales tax: 7% (Miami-Dade County, FL) applied to subtotal only
- No listing fees, no monthly subscription, no setup costs
- Stripe processes all payments (host is a Stripe Connected Account)
- Complete fee breakdown stored in fee_audit_log for every transaction

RECURRING BOOKINGS:

- Guests can request recurring weekly bookings (same day/time each week)
- Host must confirm or decline recurring booking requests
- Status tracking: pending_confirmation, confirmed, active, paused, cancelled
- Individual bookings generated from recurring schedules

POST-BOOKING:

- Guest and host can message through the platform (booking-specific and direct channels)
- Guest can check in and check out through the platform (timestamps logged)
- Overtime minutes are tracked if guest exceeds booking duration
- Guest can leave a review after completion
- Host can file a damage report within 14 days (see Section 7)
- Arrival Guide automatically sent to guest with check-in instructions

4. BOOKING AGREEMENT (IMPLEMENTED IN PRODUCT)
================================================================================

Every booking on the platform includes a Booking Agreement step before
payment. This is not a separate document but a transactional acknowledgment
layered on top of the Terms of Service.

The confirmation screen displays two sections:

"What the Host has confirmed for this booking:"

- Space is accurately described in the listing
- Space is maintained in safe, functional condition
- Host maintains active liability insurance
- Any Professional Use Certifications are accurate
- [If certified]: "This space is certified as [tier name]"

"By booking, you confirm:"

- You accept responsibility for your conduct and anyone you bring
- You are responsible for any damage to the space during your booking
- If your profession requires it, you maintain your own professional liability insurance
- You have independently verified this space meets your professional needs
- You understand Align is a marketplace facilitator and is not liable for either party's actions or the condition of the space

Guest checks a single confirmation box and proceeds to payment.

Technical implementation:

- Acceptance stored as a timestamped record tied to each booking
- Records: who accepted, when, which agreement version, IP address, user agent
- Agreement version is tracked so if language is updated, we know which version each booking was confirmed under
- Creates an audit trail for dispute resolution

5. PROFESSIONAL USE CERTIFICATIONS (IMPLEMENTED IN PRODUCT)
================================================================================

Hosts can voluntarily self-certify their workspace. Certifications are about
the SPACE, not the person booking it. Any guest can book any space regardless
of certification status.

Five tiers:

CLINICAL READY

Target: Therapists, counselors, psychologists, psychiatrists, social workers

- Private, soundproofed or sound-dampened session space
- No surveillance devices (audio or video) in session area
- Separated or private entry/waiting area, or disclosed shared arrangement
- Secure, non-shared internet connection, or disclosure of configuration
- ADA accessibility compliance

CONSULTATION READY

Target: Real estate agents, financial advisors, coaches, consultants, attorneys, accountants

- Professional, client-facing environment
- Private meeting space with closable door
- Reliable internet and power access
- Adequate seating for stated capacity

WELLNESS READY

Target: Holistic practitioners, bodyworkers, acupuncturists, yoga instructors, massage therapists

- Appropriate flooring and ventilation for stated modalities
- Adequate space dimensions for stated practice type
- Clean, sanitizable surfaces
- Private changing area or restroom access

SERVICE READY

Target: Barbers, stylists, estheticians, nail technicians, tattoo artists

- Proper ventilation per local code for stated service type
- Access to running water and sanitation facilities
- Adequate electrical capacity for professional equipment
- Easy-to-clean flooring and work surfaces
- Compliance with local health department requirements

GENERAL PROFESSIONAL

Target: Photographers, podcasters, remote workers, freelancers, tutors

- Clean, well-maintained workspace
- Reliable internet and power access
- Private or semi-private space as described in listing
- Adequate for use case stated in listing description

Implementation:

- Host selects a tier, completes checklist in listing management
- Badge appears on listing when all items are checked
- Guests can filter search results by certification type
- Disclaimer displayed: "Professional Use Certifications are self-reported by the Host and are not independently verified, audited, or endorsed by Align Workspaces."
- Align reserves the right to remove certifications for non-compliance with documented reason

6. INSURANCE VERIFICATION SYSTEM (IMPLEMENTED IN PRODUCT)
================================================================================

HOST INSURANCE (REQUIRED):

- Hard gate: no host can publish a listing without completing this step
- Data collected:
- Insurance carrier name
- Policy number
- Coverage type (General Liability, Professional Liability, Property, Business Owner's Policy, or Other)
- Coverage amount (minimum $1,000,000 general liability required)
- Policy expiration date
- Upload of declarations page (PDF or image, max 10MB)
- Documents stored securely in Cloudflare R2 object storage
- Admin can upload insurance on behalf of hosts
- Automated expiration tracking:
- Status indicators: Active (green), Expiring Soon (yellow, within 30 days), Expired (red)
- Email reminders at 30 days and 7 days before expiration (de-duplicated to prevent repeat sends)
- If policy expires without renewal: all listings automatically suspended
- "Verified Insurance" badge displayed on listings

THIMBLE PARTNERSHIP (FOR HOSTS WITHOUT COVERAGE):

- Hosts who do not have insurance are presented with an option to purchase general liability coverage through Thimble
- Thimble offers policies starting at $17/month with $1M+ coverage
- Currently implemented as a referral link; API integration pending partner approval
- Align will earn commission on policies sold through the platform

PLATFORM DISCLAIMER:

- Align does not provide insurance coverage
- Insurance Verification is limited to confirming that a host has submitted documentation that appears to reflect active coverage
- Align does not verify terms, exclusions, adequacy, or validity of any insurance policy

NOTE FOR COUNSEL:

- The $1M minimum is a platform policy, not a citation of Florida statute
- Florida does not have a specific statute requiring liability insurance to rent out workspace by the hour
- The requirement is within Align's rights as a private marketplace
- Hosts have premises liability obligations under Florida common law regardless of insurance status

7. DAMAGE REPORTING AND RESOLUTION (IMPLEMENTED IN PRODUCT)
================================================================================

REPORTING:

- "Report Issue" button available on completed bookings for 14 days after booking end time
- Host selects issue type: Property Damage, Cleanliness Issue, Policy Violation, or Other
- Host uploads photos (minimum 2, maximum 10, stored on Cloudflare R2)
- Host provides written description
- Host provides estimated repair/replacement cost (optional, stored in cents)

RESOLUTION PROCESS:

- Host submits report through platform (status: pending)
- Guest is notified through platform messaging (status: guest_notified)
- Guest has 72 hours to respond (status: guest_responded)
- If unresolved after 72 hours, report escalates to Align support (status: escalated)
- Align reviews evidence and issues determination
- Guest found responsible may be charged through payment method on file, up to documented repair or replacement cost
- If host is enrolled in Host Protection Plan, eligible claims processed under HPP terms
- Final resolution recorded with notes (status: resolved)

ALL RECORDS STORED:

- Report details, photos, timestamps
- Guest response and timestamp
- Resolution outcome and resolution notes
- Resolved by indicator (host, guest, or admin)
- Escalation timestamp
- Stored as part of the booking record for audit purposes

8. DATA AND PRIVACY CONSIDERATIONS
================================================================================

PAYMENT DATA:

- All payment processing handled by Stripe (live mode)
- Align does not store credit card numbers or bank account details
- Hosts are Stripe Connected Accounts (Stripe's onboarding and KYC)
- Full fee breakdown stored in fee_audit_log for every transaction
- Payment intent IDs, transfer IDs, and payout status tracked

USER AUTHENTICATION:

- Passwordless magic link email authentication (15-minute token expiration)
- One-time-use tokens (32-byte random hex)
- Sessions stored server-side in PostgreSQL (connect-pg-simple)
- HttpOnly secure cookies with SameSite:lax
- CSRF protection on state-changing requests
- Rate limiting on authentication endpoints
- 4 hardcoded admin email addresses for admin panel access

EMPLOYEE AUTHENTICATION:

- Bearer token format for internal team members
- Role-based permissions (editor, manager) with 13+ granular permission categories
- Employees can be deactivated/reactivated

INSURANCE DOCUMENTS:

- Declarations pages stored in Cloudflare R2 (encrypted at rest)
- Accessible only to the host and Align administrators
- Not shared with guests or other users

BOOKING AGREEMENT RECORDS:

- Timestamped with user ID, role, IP address, user agent
- Agreement version tracked
- Stored indefinitely for audit and dispute resolution purposes

MESSAGING DATA:

- Three separate messaging channels: booking messages, direct conversations, admin messages
- All messages stored in plain text (no end-to-end encryption)
- Admin has access to all user-to-user messages
- Messages can include inline images (stored on R2)
- Read timestamps tracked per participant

PUSH NOTIFICATIONS:

- Web Push API (VAPID protocol)
- Subscription endpoints, p256dh keys, and auth tokens stored
- Role-based targeting (guest/host/admin)
- Notifications sent for: new bookings, status changes, reviews, admin messages

ANALYTICS DATA:

- Page views logged with: session ID, path, duration, IP, device, browser, OS, approximate location
- Analytics events tracked with custom metadata
- Used for platform performance analysis

PROFESSIONAL PROFILES (GUEST, OPTIONAL):

- Professional title, industry, license numbers, licensing state, insurance policy numbers stored if provided
- Not displayed publicly (visible only to hosts reviewing bookings)
- Guest can delete at any time

CALENDAR INTEGRATION DATA:

- Google Calendar OAuth refresh tokens stored for hosts who enable calendar sync
- iCal feed URLs stored for external calendar imports
- External calendar blocks stored (date, time ranges, all-day flags)

IMAGE AND FILE STORAGE:

- All images stored on Cloudflare R2 (S3-compatible)
- Profile photos processed to 400x400 WebP
- Space photos processed to fit within 2400x3200 WebP
- Insurance documents (PDF/image, max 10MB)
- Damage report photos (up to 10 per report)
- Gallery images for photography service
- Arrival guide step photos
- Message attachment images

EMAIL COMMUNICATIONS:

- Transactional emails sent via Gmail API (OAuth 2.0)
- Magic link sign-in emails
- Booking confirmations and reminders
- Insurance expiration reminders (30-day and 7-day)
- Gallery ready notifications
- Invoice delivery

NEWSLETTER:

- Managed through ConvertKit/Kit API
- Subscriber data: email, first name, zip code, interests array
- Tag-based segmentation
- Subscribe/unsubscribe endpoints

================================================================================

- 24-hour grace period: full refund including platform fees

HOST-INITIATED CANCELLATION:

- Full refund to guest including all fees

NO-SHOWS:

- Non-refundable

REFUND PROCESSING:

- Refunds processed to original payment method

- Refund status tracked in booking record

ADMIN PAYOUT CONTROLS:

- Manual hold, release, and reverse capabilities
- Payouts can be held pending dispute resolution
- Payout status: pending, processing, paid, held, reversed

14. AREAS FOR LEGAL REVIEW
================================================================================

We request counsel review the following:

1. TERMS OF SERVICE (all 23 sections):

- Published at alignworkspaces.com/terms
- Particular attention to: limitation of liability, indemnification, arbitration clause, class action waiver, non-circumvention liquidated damages provision

2. PROFESSIONAL USE CERTIFICATIONS:

- Are the disclaimers sufficient to insulate Align from liability if a certified space does not meet a guest's regulatory requirements?
- Is the self-certification model legally sound, or does hosting the certification framework create implied endorsement risk?
- Are there specific Florida statutes (e.g., Ch. 476 barbering, Ch. 477 cosmetology, Ch. 480 massage, Ch. 491 mental health) that create space-specific requirements we should address?

3. INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS:

- Is the $1M minimum general liability requirement appropriate?
- Are we exposed if a host's insurance turns out to be insufficient or invalid despite our verification?
- Does our platform insurance disclaimer adequately protect us?
- Admin ability to upload insurance on behalf of hosts: does this create additional liability?

4. BOOKING AGREEMENT:

- Is the mutual acknowledgment structure legally enforceable?
- Is the digital acceptance (checkbox + timestamp + IP + user agent) sufficient evidence of agreement?
- Does the agreement adequately allocate liability between host, guest, and platform?

5. DAMAGE RESOLUTION:

- Can we legally charge a guest's payment method on file for damages based on our determination?
- What is our exposure if a damage determination is disputed?
- Is the 14-day reporting window and 72-hour response window sufficient?

6. HOST PROTECTION PLAN:

- If we self-fund a damage reserve (1-2% of service fees), does this create an insurance product requiring state licensing?
- How should we structure the HPP to avoid being classified as an insurance provider under Florida law?

7. MARKETPLACE LIABILITY:

- Under Florida law and Section 230 considerations, what is our exposure as a marketplace facilitator?
- Are there specific risks from hosting regulated professionals (HIPAA-covered therapists, licensed barbers, etc.) that we should address?
- Does admin access to all user messages affect our intermediary status?

8. ARBITRATION AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION:

- Is the binding arbitration clause enforceable in Florida?
- Is the 30-day opt-out window sufficient?
- Is the class action waiver enforceable under current Florida law?
- Does our "final determination" language on disputes create additional liability?

9. REFUND POLICY:

- Is the tiered refund structure (24-hour grace, 48-hour window, <48-hour partial) compliant with Florida consumer protection law?
- Does the no-show non-refundable policy require specific disclosure?
- Can we legally hold payouts pending dispute resolution?

10. DATA PRIVACY AND MESSAGING:

- Does admin access to all user-to-user messages require specific disclosure?
- Is plain text message storage sufficient, or should we implement encryption for regulated professional communications?
- Are we compliant with CCPA for California resident users?
- Is our data retention policy (indefinite for booking/agreement records) appropriate?
- Does collection of IP address, device info, and approximate location require specific consent?

11. RECURRING BOOKINGS:

- Does the recurring booking structure create a lease-like arrangement that could be reclassified as a lease rather than a license?
- What cancellation/modification rights should apply to recurring bookings?

12. PHOTOGRAPHY SERVICE:

- Does operating a photography service alongside the marketplace affect our intermediary status?
- Is the 50% downpayment structure enforceable?
- Does the edit token system create any consumer protection issues?

13. EMPLOYEE CLASSIFICATION:

- Team members are stored as "employees" with role-based permissions
- Bearer token authentication for team access
- Clarify employee vs. independent contractor classification

14. COMMUNITY EVENTS:

- What is our liability for user-created events hosted through the platform?
- Does the approval workflow create additional responsibility?

15. MULTI-STATE EXPANSION (FUTURE):

- What changes would be needed to operate in other states?
- Are there states with specific marketplace liability statutes we should be aware of?
- Multi-state tax collection obligations

16. REFERRAL PROGRAM:

- Does the reduced fee for referred bookings comply with anti-kickback regulations in healthcare-adjacent industries?
- Are there disclosure requirements for the referral commission structure?
